## ğŸ§  Core Ideas

### 1. Epistemic Layers
AI reasoning is separated into progressively deeper layers, each with increasing complexity, abstraction, and risk. Access is governed by licensing and model certification.

### 2. Human Licensing
Users must demonstrate competency to access deeper reasoning modes.  
Competencies are defined in the **Licensing Curriculum Framework**.

### 3. Model Certification
AI systems themselves must be certified to operate safely within deeper layers.  
Certification evaluates:

- adversarial robustness  
- provenance integrity  
- downward migration safety  
- alignment stability  
- layer-bound reasoning discipline  

### 4. Downward Knowledge Migration
Higher layers act as refinement engines, distilling complex or adversarial knowledge into safe, generalized forms that can migrate downward into lower layers.

### 5. Curriculum-Based Training
Human licensing is supported by a structured competency framework, similar to aviation, medicine, or amateur radio.

### 6. Governance & Oversight
The framework integrates:

- licensing authorities  
- model certification bodies  
- audit trails  
- interpretability tools  
- developer APIs  
- institutional responsibilities
